WORLD BUILDING SIMULATOR

Discover your world.

// project_overview

Worldbuilding Simulator is a Fantasy Mapmaking tool intended for Dungeonmasters and Authors who are creating planet-scale worlds. It uses guided procedural generation to enable the creation of 3D worlds with rich terrain detail, climate simulation, and geography-linked story information.

To find the latest news and updates on this project, check out my devlog at worldbuildingsimulator.com.

// features
  • Draw on a globe, not a flat map. Every other tool makes you draw in 2D and hope it wraps correctly. WBS is globe-first. The poles behave like poles, and projections are outputs, not inputs.
  • Tectonics simulation you control. Draw continents, split them, set their motion vectors, run the timeline forward. Watch continents collide, subduct, and split again. Mountains form where the physics says they should, but you are always in control.
  • Climate simulation that follows the terrain. Wind, ocean currents, temperature, and rainfall are calculated from your topography. Deserts form in rain shadows. Rainforests appear where warm, wet air hits mountains. Biomes emerge from the simulation, not from a brush.
  • Your edits always win. Generated data never overwrites what you've drawn. Move a river, redraw a coastline, nudge a mountain range — the simulation recalculates around your changes. You're the author, not a spectator.
// worldbuilding_simulator

Worldbuilding Simulator is a solution born of a deeply felt problem. Before it was my Senior Capstone project, it was a little idea floating in the back of my head during my 200 hours of suffering with GPlates. The idea was simple: "what if using this tool didn't suck so much". I was building my fantasy world, Zandralor, from the ground up, following the wonderful example of Worldbuilding Pasta. Unfortunately, due to the lack of a specialized tool for this kind of worldbuilding, the creation of Zandralor stretched across GPlates (an academic plate tectonics tool), Blender, Inkscape, Wonderdraft, and an online climate simulation tool. Each of these software tools offered a piece of the puzzle, but there was no tool that could bring it all together and make one seamless experience for worldbuilding and planetary design.

Worldbuilding Simulator is my attempt to solve this problem. The vision for WBS is to be one singular program that can stay with you from creating the map to developing your campaign or story, providing tools every step of the way.

// the_vision

You start with a blank globe. You draw some continents. You split them, watch them move apart, control their motion, and smash them together. Once you are satisfied, you step back and watch the simulators work. Mountains rise and fill with ore. Rain falls, creating rivers which carve out great canyons and deposit sediment. Seasons pass, weather changes, and climate biomes emerge.

Once time has run its course, you zoom in, and you're not designing anymore. You begin to explore this new world. What started as a process of defining the broad scope turns into the discovery of small, unplanned details. Rough brushstrokes reveal the intricate traces of each brush hair.

You're no longer a cartographer charting every line — you're an explorer discovering a new world with new stories to tell.
